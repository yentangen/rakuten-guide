To further understand the power of point systems, let's take a look at my personal, non-Rakuten example. I live on the 東急東横 (Tokyu Toyoko) line, and my workplace requires me to transfer to a Metro line. I apply for two credit cards: one is a Mastercard Tokyu ClubQ + ANA + Pasmo card (Tokyu card from now on), and the other is a JCB Metro To-Me Prime card (Metro card from now on). The Metro card allows me to be a To-Me member, which I have assigned my Pasmo from the Tokyu card to it. Lastly, for every 1000JPY I spend I also receive 1 ワールドプレゼント (World Present) point from SMBC Bank.

Here's the rundown of the points I receive:
- Purchasing a 定期券 (commuter pass) using Tokyu card: 1pt Tokyu for every 100JPY + 1pt WP for every 1000JPY of the amount
- Riding Tokyu line to work: 10pt Tokyu daily (have to redeem at a kiosk in a station I pass by everyday)
- Riding Metro line to work: 10pt Metro daily (20pt if I use on weekend) automatically

As you can see, the above three points do not require me to spend a single Yen of my money (commuter fee reimbursed by company). This doesn't count the things that I spend money for, such as Pasmo Autocharge service, where whenever my PASMO runs under 2000JPY it charges another 3000, billing my Tokyu card which gives me 30pt Tokyu and 3pt WP. For any places that take CC, I make sure to use it unless it charges extra, and I use Pasmo for everywhere else. These three types of points (Tokyu, Metro, World Present) can all be used to convert to ANA mileage points (which is part of the Tokyu Card) to fund my next vacation in First Class :-).
